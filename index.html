How this is calculated

  • Disbursement: the loan is released in yearly tranches across the construction period. Use Even split for equal tranches (e.g. 4-year possession = 25%/year) or switch to Custom to set the exact percentage released at the start of each year. Immediate possession releases 100% upfront.
  • Pre-EMI: until possession you pay only interest on the amount disbursed so far — so it starts small and rises with each tranche. Principal is untouched in this phase.
  • EMI: the full reducing-balance EMI begins at possession and runs for the chosen tenure. EMI = P·r·(1+r)n / ((1+r)n−1).
  • Prepayments hit the outstanding principal in the year you set, then either shorten the tenure (EMI fixed) or lower the EMI (tenure fixed), per your choice.

Estimates only. Banks compute on daily/monthly reducing balance with their own rounding, and may add processing fees, insurance, GST and stamp duty (not modelled here). Floating rates change with the RBI repo rate. Confirm exact figures with your lender. This is not financial advice.

How outflow is split

  • From salary (recurring, paid from monthly income): household living expenses (excluding rent, rising by the household hike each year), rent until possession, and the loan payment — pre-EMI during construction, full EMI after.
  • From savings (lumpy, paid from your corpus): the down payment released in step with the loan tranches, plus the house-buying charges — GST, registration/stamp duty and overhead applied to the house price as a one-time hit at possession, and any lump-sum prepayments.

GST, registration/stamp duty and incidental overhead are house-purchase charges that vary by state, project type and date — simplified placeholders applied to the full house price at possession. Household is general living expense, separate from the home. Verify actual rates locally. Not financial advice.

How net worth is tracked

  • Inflow each month = salary (growing yearly until you retire, then zero) plus any one-off bonus/RSU landing that month. The timeline runs past loan payoff to ~10 years beyond retirement so you can see whether the corpus sustains household spend once the salary stops.
  • Outflow each month = household expense (excluding rent, growing yearly) plus everything from the Money-outflow tab (rent, EMI/pre-EMI, down-payment, charges, prepayments).
  • Liquid (cash) starts at your corpus, compounds monthly at the corpus return, and rises or falls by each month's surplus or deficit. Months where it turns negative are flagged red — you'd be out of cash.
  • Home equity: while the home is under construction it's just the money you've paid in (down payments — recoverable at par, no gain). From possession it becomes the sell-&-settle value: market value (appreciating from booking) minus the outstanding loan. Total net worth = liquid cash + home equity, so the table reconciles exactly: Total = Liquid + Home equity. Down payments and EMI principal shift cash into equity rather than destroying it; only interest, rent, charges and household spend are true losses.

Highly simplified: one blended return on all savings, no tax on income/gains, no emergency buffer, growth applied in yearly steps. "In the red" tracks liquid cash only — total net worth can look healthy while you're still cash-poor, which is why both lines matter. For planning intuition, not financial advice.
